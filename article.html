GTA 6 Preload Guide: Dates, File Size and How to Be Ready for Launch

Guide By the Vice Countdown Team · Published Jul 17, 2026 · 4 min read

Grand Theft Auto VI launches on November 19, 2026 — and if you want to be playing the moment it unlocks, preload is how you get there. Digital preload is scheduled to begin on November 12, 2026, a full week ahead of release. Here's what that actually means and how to prepare.

What is preload, and why does it matter?

Preload lets you download the full game to your console before it officially unlocks. The files sit locked on your drive until launch day, then unlock at release. For a game the size of GTA 6, that difference can be hours: without preload, you'd be starting a very large download at the exact moment millions of other players are doing the same thing, on servers under heavy load.

Key dates

  • November 12, 2026 — digital preload begins.
  • November 19, 2026 — launch day on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.

These dates come from Rockstar's official announcements. Treat any earlier "secret unlock" time you see on social media as unverified.

Digital vs physical

If you pre-ordered a digital copy from the PlayStation Store, Xbox Store or Rockstar Store, preload should appear on your console around November 12 — just start the download and let it finish before launch.

The physical edition is a little different: the box contains a download code, not a disc. Once you redeem that code, the Standard edition can also preload ahead of launch. So even the "physical" version is really a digital install — plan to redeem your code as soon as it arrives rather than waiting until launch morning.

Neither the Standard nor the Ultimate edition unlocks the game early. Preload only gets the files onto your console; the game still becomes playable for everyone at the same launch moment.

How big will the download be?

Rockstar has not published an official file size yet, so be sceptical of specific numbers floating around. What's realistic to plan for: GTA 6 is a flagship, current-generation open-world game, so expect a large download — comfortably in the tens of gigabytes and quite possibly around the ~100 GB range that modern AAA titles often reach. The safe move is simply to free up plenty of storage in advance so space isn't what stops you at launch.

Your pre-launch checklist

  • Clear storage now. Delete or archive games you're not playing so there's ample room.
  • Redeem physical codes early. Don't wait until launch day to open the box.
  • Check your internet. A wired connection makes a big download far more reliable than Wi-Fi.
  • Start the preload on November 12 (or as soon as it appears) and let it finish well before the 19th.
  • Keep your console updated so a surprise system update doesn't eat launch-day time.

Bottom line

Preload turns launch day from "wait for a huge download" into "press play." Mark November 12 to start it, make sure you have the space, and you'll be in Vice City the moment the countdown hits zero on November 19, 2026.

Dates sourced from Rockstar Newswire and official storefront listings. File-size figures are not officially confirmed and are given only as general planning guidance. Any other unlock time or size circulating on social media should be treated as unverified.
